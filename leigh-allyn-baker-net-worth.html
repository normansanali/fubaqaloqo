    Leigh-Allyn Baker Wiki Biography

    Leigh-Allyn Baker was born on the 13th March 1972, in Murray, Kentucky USA, and is an actress and voice artist, probably best known for her role as Amy Duncan in the TV series “Good Luck Charlie” (2010-2014), and also in the movie “Good Luck Charlie, It’s Christmas!” (2011). Baker’s career started in 1994.

    Have you ever wondered how rich Leigh-Allyn Baker is as of late 2016? According to authoritative sources, it has been estimated that Baker’s net worth is as high as $2 million, an amount earned through her successful acting career. In addition to appearing on television and in films, Baker has also given her voice to numerous characters on video games, and these have improved her wealth too.

    Leigh-Allyn Baker Net Worth $2 Million

    Leigh-Allyn Baker is the daughter of Vicki and Mike Baker and has a brother, Chuck Baker, who is an owner and Chief Executive Officer of Kentucky Lake Oil Company.

    Baker started her acting career in 1994 in Richard Elfman’s comedy-horror called “Shrunken Heads’, and later gave her voice to Alisa Silvermane in “Spider-Man” from 1995 to 1997. She had minor roles in TV series “Almost Perfect” (1996), “The Last Frontier” (1996), and “Fired Up” (1997), before appearing in the TV movie “Breast Men” (1997) starring David Schwimmer, Chris Cooper, and Emily Procter. Baker ended the ‘90s with parts in “Inner Shadow” (1997), “A Wake in Providence” (1999), “Family Law” (1999), and also played Hannah Webster in five episodes of “Charmed” (1998-1999). Leigh-Allyn had the recurring role of Ellen in 19 episodes of “Will & Grace” from 1998 to 2006, and that gained her a lot of popularity, and also increased her net worth.

    In the early 2000s, Baker appeared in such movies as “Very Mean Men” (2000) with Matthew Modine, Martin Landau, and Ben Gazzara, “Frozen Impact” (2003), and “Triple Play” (2004) alongside Zac Efron, increasing further her net worth. She also worked in numerous video games including “Star Trek Voyager: Elite Force” (2000), “Command & Conquer: Renegade” (2002), “Star Trek: Elite Force II” (2003), and “Fatal Frame II: Crimson Butterfly” (2003), and continued with “X-Men Legends” (2004), “EverQuest II” (2004), “Law & Order: Criminal Intent” (2005), and “X-Men Legends II: Rise of Apocalypse” (2005). In the mid-2000s, Baker had parts in such series as “Las Vegas” (2005), “Boston Legal” (2005), “House” (2006), “The King of Queens” (2006), and “My Name Is Earl” (2007), and also played in five episodes of “In Case of Emergency” (2007), gave her voice to various characters in “Back at the Barnyard” (2007-2011), and appeared in six episodes of “12 Miles of Bad Road” (2008). Her net worth rose steadily.

    In 2010, Baker got a leading role in the family-comedy TV series called “Good Luck Charlie”, playing Amy Duncan in 97 episodes over four seasons after securing the role even though she was nine months pregnant at the time. Leigh-Allyn played Amy in the movie “Good Luck Charlie, It’s Christmas!” (2011) and starred in a comedy “Bad Hair Day” (2015) with Laura Marano. Most recently, Baker worked in “Little Savages” (2016) and “Wish for Christmas” (2016), while her next movies “Swiped’ and “Take 2 for Faith” are in post-production and will be released in the late 2016 and early 2017, respectively.

    Regarding her personal life, Leigh-Allyn Baker married an entertainment executive, Keith James Kauffman, in 2005, and she has two sons with him.
