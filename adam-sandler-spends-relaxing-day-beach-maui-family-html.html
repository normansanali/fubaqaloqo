    It looks like another day in paradise for Adam Sandler.

    The 47-year-old comedian and wife Jackie were spotted deep in conversation on the beach on the Hawaiian island of Maui on Monday.

    The couple have been on holiday for around a week with their two children, Sadie, seven, and five-year-old Sunny. But there was no sign of the little girls.

    It's a tough life: Adam Sandler and wife Jackie, who have spent Christmas in Hawaii with their two daughters, were back on the beach in Maui on Monday

    It appears that Adam didn't bring many clothes with him as he was wearing the same outfit he'd been seen in on Christmas Eve.

    He looked happy and relaxed in a baggy white T-shirt from Boca Surf & Sail, blue and white patchwork board shorts,  a pair of plastic-framed blue sunglasses, a green baseball cap and Puma trainers.

    Strangely, Jackie, 39, was almost completely covered up from the sun in black exercise leggings, a black long-sleeved T-shirt and black trainers.

    Talking something out: The couple seemed deep in conversation as they stood on the sand

    The former model-turned-actress had her long brunette tresses pulled back into a low ponytail.

    Adam and Jackie have been together for a decade after meeting on the set of Big Daddy in which she played a waitress.

    The Saturday Night Live veteran is filming The Cobbler in New York and managed to fit in the family holiday during a break in production for the Christmas season.

    Adam plays a shoe repairman who metaphysically slips into the lives of people whose shoes he fixes.

    That looks familiar: The 47-year-old funnyman was first seen in this casual get up on Christmas Eve

    The star has also started on the upcoming film, Men, Women & Children, about a group of high school teens and parents trying to navigate the many ways the Internet has changed their lives.

    Adam also recently wrapped the comedy Blended, co-starring Drew Barrymore. The film is due to hit cinemas in the US and UK on May 22.

    Meanwhile, the Happy Gilmore star was recently named 2013's most overpaid actor by Forbes, which claims he is one of the few actors in Hollywood who can still demand a pay check of around $15 million.
