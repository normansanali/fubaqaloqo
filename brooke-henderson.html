    Biography Timeline

    Brooke Henderson is a Canadian professional golfer who has made a name for herself internationally. She was born on September 10, 1997, in Smiths Falls, Ontario, Canada. Henderson began playing golf at a young age and quickly gained attention for her talent. Since becoming a professional in 2014, she has won numerous tournaments and become a role model for young golfers worldwide.

    2008: Henderson wins the Ontario Junior Girls’ Championship at 10.

    2012: Henderson becomes the youngest player to win the Canadian Women’s Amateur Championship at 14.

    2014: Henderson turns professional and begins playing on the Symetra Tour.

    2015: Henderson earns her first LPGA Tour victory at the Cambia Portland Classic.

    2016: Henderson wins the KPMG Women’s PGA Championship, becoming the first Canadian woman to win a major golf championship since 1968.

    2017: Henderson wins two LPGA Tour events, the Meijer LPGA Classic and the McKayson New Zealand Women’s Open.

    2018: Henderson wins the Lotte Championship and becomes the winningest Canadian golfer on the LPGA Tour.

    2019: Henderson wins the Meijer LPGA Classic for the second time, becoming the first Canadian golfer to win the same LPGA tournament twice.

    2021: Henderson wins the Hugel-Air Premia LA Open, marking her 10th LPGA Tour victory.
