    Meet Stacy Kravetz, wife of OBGYN Jay Goldberg.

    Jay Goldberg, a celebrity Hollywood doctor, has died while hiking in a Los Angeles park, according to New York Post. He was working as an OBGYN at a Cedar-Sinai’s Beverly Hills and was found dead Sunday by first responders inside Will Rogers State Park, according to Fox News.

    As reported, he died of a heart attack at the age of 53. He was married to Stacy Kravetz. Learn more about his wife in this article below.

    Meet OBGYN Jay Goldberg Wife, Stacy Kravetz

    Stacy Kravetz was married to OBGYN Jay Goldberg at the time of his death. Not much is known about their relationship and how it all began, but they were likely married for more than 15 years.

    After his death, Stacy wrote a statement and shared, “Jay was loved by so many. His sense of humor was legendary, and he never missed an opportunity to make others feel better, lighter, more loved, and more comfortable. Jay epitomized what it means to be a mensch. He was loving, easygoing, funny, and sweet. He leaves a tremendous void in the hearts of everyone who knew him”.

    Jay’s friend who paid tribute to him and his wife shared that Jay boasted of his children and his wife. He would often mention how busy Stacy was writing her book.

    Other than working for Cedars-Sinai, he also ran a clinic named Women’s Care of Beverley Hills which he opened in November 2002. He had 26 years long career and had received a Board Certified in Obstetrics and Gynecology.

    Besides his beloved wife Stacy, Jay Goldberg was survived by their two children; Oliver and Jesse Goldberg. Jesse, who turned 23 on 1 June 2022, loved basketball. He is also available on Facebook (@jesse.goldberg.965).

    As for Oliver, besides a few pictures on their parent’s socials, not much is known about Oliver. But, like his brother, Oliver is also available on Facebook (@oliver.goldberg.75).

    Stacy Kravetz Age

    Reportedly born in January 1968, Stacy Kravetz is 54 years old.

    Stacy Kravetz Job

    Stacy Kravetz is a successful writer and published author of many non-fiction and young adult books. She started her career as a staff reporter for The Wall Street Journal and has contributed to The New York Times, The Los Angeles Times, and other newspapers.

    The first book she wrote was, Welcome to the Real World: You’ve Got an Education—Now Get a Life!. She wrote her first book in her 20s she was a wide-eyed college graduate with very little clue about how to manage her working life and my actual life.

    Among other works, Stacy is the author of the bestseller Girl Boss: Running the Show Like the Big Chicks;  She’s So Boss, and The Fosters: Keep Your Frenemies Close.

    Stacy also wrote a book titled The Dating Race. Speaking on the book she briefly wrote, “This is an older book, one that came out of many years of hapless dating, when I started to wonder what we were all doing out there, blind dating our way into oblivion and not finding “the one.”

    She continued writing, “It made me wonder how we’d evolved – or devolved – from the delicate rituals of waltzing from partner to partner to speed dating around the room at the blast of a whistle. This was my undercover project aimed at finding out. From speed dating to dating experts, there is a billion dollar industry around our efforts to find each other in an ever-complicated world”.

    Stacy stated in her bio, “When I’m not madly scrawling words on a page, I’m fueling up on coffee before running or cycling in the canyons. In addition to non-fiction books, I am beginning a career as a fiction author. Stay tuned”.

    Other than books, Stacy also contributed as a writer for TV shows. According to her IMDB bio, she wrote two episodes of South of Nowhere (2005-2007), and four episodes of Cold Case (2003-2004).

    She received her B.A. in Political Economies of Industrialized Societies at the University of California, Berkeley. After that, she earned her M.A. in Journalism at the University of Southern California.
