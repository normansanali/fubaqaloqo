    Remembering Peter Schickele

    In remembrance of Peter Schickele, we honor his remarkable contributions to the world of music. Schickele’s comedic genius and musical talent brought laughter and delight to audiences worldwide. Through his alter ego, P.D.Q. Bach, he showcased his ability to seamlessly blend classical music with humor, leaving a lasting impact on the industry. Beyond his parodies, Schickele’s work as a composer and musical educator has left an indelible mark on the music community.

    Death and Age

    It is with heavy hearts that we announce the passing of Peter Schickele at the age of 88. While we mourn the loss of this musical legend, we take solace in knowing that his musical legacy will continue to resonate for generations to come. Schickele’s compositions and performances captivated audiences throughout his career, and his absence will be deeply felt by all who had the privilege of experiencing his talent. Though he may no longer be with us physically, his music will forever live on in our hearts and minds.

    Accomplishments and Career

    P.D.Q. Bach

    Peter Schickele’s illustrious career as a composer, musical educator, and parodist is marked by a multitude of accomplishments and contributions to the world of music. One of his most notable achievements was the creation of the fictional composer P.D.Q. Bach. Through this alter ego, Schickele showcased his comedic genius and musical prowess. The whimsical and satirical compositions attributed to P.D.Q. Bach brought laughter and joy to listeners, while also highlighting Schickele’s deep understanding of classical music.

    Grammy Awards

    Schickele’s talent and creativity were not only recognized but celebrated by the music industry. His P.D.Q. Bach recordings earned him four consecutive Grammy Awards for Best Comedy Album from 1990 to 1993. This prestigious accolade not only solidified Schickele’s status as a comedic musical genius but also showcased his ability to connect with audiences on a profound level. His ability to blend humor and music in a way that resonated with listeners was truly unparalleled.

    Collaborations and Film Score

    In addition to his solo work, Schickele collaborated with various artists and musicians throughout his career. Notably, he worked with folk icon Joan Baez, orchestrating and arranging three albums for her. His musical contributions extended beyond the realm of comedy and parody. Schickele’s versatility as a composer was further showcased through his work on the 1972 science fiction film Silent Running, where he created captivating and evocative scores. His ability to adapt his musical style to different genres and projects demonstrated his immense talent and versatility.

    Chamber Rock Trio


    Schickele’s musical talents extended beyond composition and parody. He was also a member of the chamber rock trio, the Open Window. Together, they wrote and performed music for the 1969 revue Oh! Calcutta! and released three albums. This collaboration allowed Schickele to explore different musical styles and push the boundaries of traditional genres. His involvement in the chamber rock trio showcased his ability to adapt and experiment with various musical forms, further solidifying his reputation as a multifaceted musician.

    Influence and Humorous Aspect

    One of the defining aspects of Schickele’s musical career was his ability to infuse humor into his compositions. Inspired by the comedic musical ensemble Spike Jones, Schickele’s early interest in parody and satire laid the foundation for his unique style. His annual humorous concerts, which eventually led to the creation of P.D.Q. Bach, became immensely popular and showcased his ability to entertain and engage audiences through laughter and music. Schickele’s influence on the music world extended beyond his comedic endeavors, as his ability to blend humor and music challenged traditional notions of what classical music could be.
